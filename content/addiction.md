Nowadays, AI is everywhere especially in code.
Plenty has already been said about this. People taking sides on if is good or bad, if its worth or not, if it makes you more productive or not.
I just wanna talk about the adiction side of it.
From my perspective these things are addictive as hell, regardless if the output is good or bad.

I saw this meme comparing vibe coding and gambling and the comparison holds surprisingly well.
First, to have the machine do anything you have to put in money.
You input your little prompt and click Accept all changes.
The result its not quite what you want. You edit your input and throw a little more money and spin again.
And you keep on this cycle.

And all the rest that we usually associate with gambling is true.
You have a casino that always wins, the model provider.
You have this idea that its easier this way, easy win phalacy.
You might even have your own special strategy to always get the best results, its not counting cards, its prompt engineering.
You loose track of time and every hour and minute seems like the perfect time to be prompting your next big idea.
You try to cram every living moment with this technology? doing chores and prompting? grocery shopping and prompting?

If this hits too close to home, you're addicted. Go touch some grass.
If you are working now more than before, because this has unblocked you or some bullshit like that, you're just addicted. Go kick some rocks.
If every task that you have to do now, you reach for this tool in search for answers, you're addicted. Be normal for a second.

I've been here. I've been this person, and sometimes I still am.

But I think its still worth to question, if reaching for these tools is actually the most productive thing to do or if we are just all hooked on the juice. For real, are these things actually good or are we just waiting for the next hit?

Yes, when I come home with my megalonomous stupid side project idea that has been rattling around my head all day and claude code is able to spew out 90% of the code out in a 15K tokens session, it feels great.
When I want it to make a small change, and suddenly its rewriting the whole app design again from scratch, or reading the whole repo from scratch again for the 9th time, it feels awkard.
When he miss undertood you and was pushin in the wrong direction for the last 7k tokens. It just feels plain bad and annoying. Maybe it should have stayed rattling on my mind for a little longer.

Recently, I find myself constantly wondering this.

All the questions that I ask comes back with another question. I always have to try and constantly bend these things to my will, fighting all the way through. Please, don't make mistakes, think before you act, be concise. Even when using the same tool and working the same way as always the results are always unpredictable. In the domains where I know a thing or two I'm always slightly underwhelmed, so in the domains where I think its extraordinary, is it really amazing, or is that I just don't know more?

Like, how can we take these companies for real?
They are creating tools to generate more code, and then also now creating tools to review this code? 
It like the company selling cakes, is now also selling the fat loss drug? What are we even doing?
I feel like we are all getting hooked on the new tobacco.

But yet I return, pay the subcription and get another hit.
